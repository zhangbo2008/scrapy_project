Since December 2015, Tiangong-1 has been falling as it gradually brushes against the upper atmosphere.\r\n\r\n\r\nTracking the Station\r\n\r\n\r\nTiangong-1 orbits Earth in a band between 43 degrees north and 43 degrees south latitudes. This was the last crew to visit Tiangong-1.\r\n\r\n\r\nLooking Down\r\n\r\n\r\nWhile it was operational, Tiangong-1 used several instruments to observe the Earth.\r\n\r\n\r\nFuture Plans\r\n\r\n\r\nChina launched its second space station, Tiangong-2, in September 2016.
